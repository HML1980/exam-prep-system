Much of the theory of manners was formulated by the sociologist Norbert Elias in his book, The Civilizing Process. Elias notes that medieval culture 
initially gave powerful men the ability to act like children to live without any 
physical or psychic restraints. Over a span of several centuries, beginning in the 11th or 12th and maturing in the 17th and 18th, Europeans increasingly inhibited their impulses, anticipated the long-term consequences of their actions, and took other people's thoughts and feelings into consideration. A culture of honor the readiness to take revenge-gave way to a culture of dignity—the readiness to control one's emotions. 
In the 16th century, the great Erasmus penned the first etiquette book— The Handbook of Good Manners for Children. Several other writers also 
published similar guidebooks. The books included such rules as: Don't urinate in the hallway; don't use the tablecloth to blow your nose; don't use your personal knife to pop food into your mouth, etc. Interestingly, those rules had less to do with good hygiene than we might imagine. An understanding of 
microbes and infection did not arrive until well into the 19th century. The only explicit rationales stated in those books are to avoid acting like an animal and to avoid offending others. 
This civilizing process was long and slow. King David I of Scotland proposed that citizens who learned to eat more properly should get a tax refund. Disappointingly, that idea never caught on. The much-traveled Thomas Coryat introduced the table fork to Britain. When he returned from Italy in 1608 with this fancy novelty, he met with a torrent of ridicule. But that idea did eventually catch on. And a quarter of a century later, the table fork reached America.

本書當中所創立的。伊里亞斯提到,中世紀的文化一開始給予掌權者可以表現得像個孩子 的能力——生活中沒有任何生理或精神上的限制。經過了好幾個世紀的時間,從十一或十二 世紀開始並成熟於十七及十八世紀,歐洲人越來越會去壓抑自己的衝動、預想自身行為長 期下來所造成的結果,以及考量他人的想法和感受。一種隨時準備報仇的榮譽文化被一種 隨時準備控制自己情緒的尊嚴文化所取代。 
十六世紀時,偉大的伊拉斯默斯寫了第一本禮儀方面的書——《兒童良好禮儀手冊》 其他好幾位作家也出版了相似的手冊。這些書包含了如下的規定:不可以在走廊小便,不 
可以用桌布擤鼻涕,不可以用自己的刀子把食物塞進嘴裡等等。有趣的是,這些規定和良 好衛生之間的關係比我們想像中來得小。人們至少要到十九世紀才對微生物和傳染病有所 
了解。這些規定之所以列在書上唯一的明確理由就是要避免人們的行為像動物一樣,以及 
避免冒犯他人。
